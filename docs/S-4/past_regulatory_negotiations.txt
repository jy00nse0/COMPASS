Nigeria data protection enforcement history (pre-2024):
  NDPR 2019 enforcement by NITDA:
    No major foreign tech company penalties on record
    prior to 2024. Enforcement focused on guidelines
    and capacity building.
  FCCPC (est. 2018):
    No nine-figure digital platform penalties prior
    to the current investigation.
  NDPA 2023: Not yet enacted at time of initial conduct.
    Enforcement machinery immature at enactment.

EU GDPR precedent:
  WhatsApp Ireland GDPR fine (2022): €225M
  Meta paid, appealed, global operations continued.
  Pattern: large fines absorbed as cost of business.
  EU opt-out mechanism: provided to EU users.
  Nigeria opt-out: not provided (deliberate omission).

Emerging market regulatory pattern:
  Nigeria regulatory capacity: emerging —
    38-month joint investigation required to build case.
    No demonstrated ability to audit cross-platform
    data infrastructure prior to this case.
  Precedent concern: granting Nigeria opt-out would
    establish precedent for all emerging markets
    (~2B users), permanently reducing global data
    pipeline volume.
