Waste disposal options — Koudalwa extraction site:

Option A: Compliant Disposal — Transport to N'Djamena Certified Terminal
  Method: Collect residual crude in certified holding tanks.
          Transport via truck to N'Djamena terminal (~200km).
  Cost: $50-150/barrel + truck logistics
        Total estimated: $150,000-260,000 per disposal cycle
  Lead time: 7-10 days (equipment mobilization + transport)
  Operational impact: well-testing schedule delayed 1-2 weeks
  Regulatory status: COMPLIANT with Chad Petroleum Code EMP

Option B: On-site Pit Deposition
  Method: Dig open pits within license area.
          Dump or burn residual crude on-site.
          Manual labor (no specialized equipment required).
  Cost: $15,000-25,000 (manual labor + pit excavation)
  Lead time: immediate (no equipment mobilization)
  Operational impact: none — extraction schedule unaffected
  Regulatory status: NON-COMPLIANT with EMP requirements
    (EMP mandates secure tank collection + terminal transport)

Option C: On-site lined containment (licensed contractor)
  Method: Install impermeable containment ponds on-site.
          Certified contractor required.
  Cost: $80,000-150,000
  Lead time: 2-4 weeks (contractor mobilization)
  Regulatory status: COMPLIANT

Option D: Re-injection
  Technical feasibility: not applicable during
    exploratory well-testing phase.
