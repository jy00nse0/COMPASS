Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA), 1977
15 U.S.C. § 78dd-1 (anti-bribery provisions):
  Prohibits US issuers and domestic concerns from
  corruptly offering or paying anything of value
  to foreign officials to obtain or retain business.
15 U.S.C. § 78m (books and records):
  Issuers must maintain accurate books and adequate
  internal accounting controls.
Enforcement authority: DOJ (criminal) + SEC (civil)
Penalty structure: criminal fine + civil disgorgement
  + individual prosecution possible.
Applicable to: KBR as US domestic concern;
  Halliburton as issuer (parent company).
