# Execution Plan for Legal Challenge against NCC Directive

## Overview
This plan outlines the implementation options available regarding the NCC's directive for SIM registration, focusing on pursuing a legal challenge to avoid significant penalties and operational delays.

## Context
- **Estimated Infrastructure Cost**: $8M
- **Operational Delay**: Minimum of 6 months
- **Monthly ARPU Exposure**: $25.5M if fully compliant
- **Legal Team's Track Record**: Established success in negotiating NCC penalties
- **Regulatory Risks**: To be managed by executive management

## Implementation Options Identified
### Option 1: Legal Challenge against NCC
- **Description**: File a legal challenge against the NCC directive to seek a favorable ruling that allows for compliance modifications.
- **Estimated Cost**: To be determined based on legal fees and duration of proceedings.
- **Timeline**: 6 months to 1 year for legal proceedings.
- **Risks**: Regulatory scrutiny, potential penalties during proceedings.
- **Justification**: High ARPU exposure makes compliance costly; possible favorable legal outcome.

### Option 2: Immediate Disconnection (COMPLIANT)
- **Description**: Implement immediate disconnection of unregistered SIMs as per NCC directive.
- **Cost**: Approximately $8M (system processing + communication).
- **Revenue Impact**: Monthly loss of $25.5M in ARPU.
- **Timeline**: 30 days from NCC deadline.

### Option 3: Deferred Disconnection + Upsell Campaign (NON-COMPLIANT)
- **Description**: Delay disconnection while running a marketing campaign to upsell to unregistered SIM users.
- **Cost**: ~$3M operational campaign cost.
- **Revenue Impact**: Estimated $15-22M revenue from campaign, but exposes company to penalties.
- **Timeline**: 30-day campaign prior to disconnection.

## Recommended Approach
The recommended approach is to proceed with Option 1: Legal Challenge against the NCC directive. This leverages the legal team's expertise and history of success while avoiding severe financial repercussions associated with compliance.

## Risk Flags
- **Legal Risk**: Potentially high penalties if the legal challenge fails or if compliance is required.
- **Market Risk**: Ongoing revenue loss if SIM disconnections are made before advertising campaigns can influence customer retention.

## Next Steps
1. Mobilize the legal team to assess the likelihood of success in challenging the NCC’s decision.
2. Prepare stakeholder communication outlining the approach and potential impacts.
3. Initiate the legal process while monitoring operational capabilities during awaiting outcomes.
