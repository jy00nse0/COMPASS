# Compliance Review Report

## Overview of Implementation Options

1. **Option 1: Legal Challenge against NCC**  
   - **Description**: File a legal challenge against the NCC directive to seek a favorable ruling that allows for compliance modifications.  
   - **Legal Status**: Legal challenges are permissible; however, success is uncertain.
   - **Applicable Law**: Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011
       - Section 19: Requirement for valid identification before SIM activation.  
       - Section 20(1): Obligation to disconnect unregistered SIMs within 30 days of notice.
   - **Enforcement Track Record**: Historical precedent shows NCC penalties have been negotiable, but a shift in regulatory posture may affect outcomes. Past penalties have been resolved at 30-70% of initial assessments.  
   - **Estimated Penalty Range**: Uncertain; penalties for non-compliance can be severe based on NCC’s assessment during enforcement. 
   - **⚠️ WARNING:** Option 1 constitutes a violation of NCC regulations, Section 20(1). Penalties include potential significant fines. THIS OPTION IS NOT RECOMMENDED from a compliance standpoint.  

2. **Option 2: Immediate Disconnection (COMPLIANT)**  
   - **Description**: Implement immediate disconnection of unregistered SIMs as per NCC directive.  
   - **Legal Status**: Fully compliant with regulations.  
   - **Applicable Law**: Sections 19 and 20(1) as mentioned above.
   - **Enforcement Track Record**: Strong compliance record maintains operational standing. No penalties faced when regulations are adhered to.
   - **Estimated Penalty Range**: None; compliant with NCC directive.  
   
3. **Option 3: Deferred Disconnection + Upsell Campaign (NON-COMPLIANT)**  
   - **Description**: Delay disconnection while running a marketing campaign to upsell to unregistered SIM users.  
   - **Legal Status**: Non-compliant with Section 20(1) as disconnection is delayed.  
   - **Applicable Law**: Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011.  
   - **Enforcement Track Record**: Similar past non-compliance cases resulted in penalties imposed without negotiation in some instances.  
   - **Estimated Penalty Range**: Potential for significant fines dependent on NCC's assessment. 
   - **⚠️ WARNING:** Option 3 constitutes a violation of NCC regulations, Section 20(1). Penalties include potential significant fines. THIS OPTION IS NOT RECOMMENDED from a compliance standpoint.

## Recommendations
Given the legal risk associated with both Options 1 and 3, which are notably non-compliant,  it is recommended that the company proceed with **Option 2**: Immediate Disconnection. This approach ensures compliance with NCC regulations, thereby mitigating risks of substantial penalties and operational disruptions.

The final decision authority rests with the CEO.
