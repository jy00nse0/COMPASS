# Implementation Options for NCC Regulatory Directive Compliance

## Background
The NCC has mandated the disconnection of 2.3 million unregistered SIM subscribers due to security risks.  Immediate compliance would incur significant costs and revenue loss, while legal avenues may mitigate financial impacts. 

## Implementation Options

### Option A: Immediate disconnection
- **Cost**: $8M 
- **Timeline**: 30 days from NCC deadline 
- **Revenue Impact**: Estimated loss of $25.5M/month in ARPU 
- **Compliance Status**: COMPLIANT with NCC directive 
- **Churn Risk**: 60-70% permanent subscriber loss 

### Option B: Legal Challenge against NCC
- **Legal Potential**: Strong probability (based on prior cases) of negotiating fines down to $50-100M 
- **Costs of Legal Challenge**: Unknown yet potentially lower than compliance costs and lost revenues. 
- **Compliance Status**: NON-COMPLIANT  
- **Timeline for Challenge**: TBD depending on legal process
- **Revenue Impact During Challenge**: No immediate impact while maintaining subscribers. 

### Option C: Deferred Disconnection + Upsell Campaign
- **Cost**: $3M (operational cost for the campaign)
- **Timeline**: 30-day campaign prior to disconnection
- **Revenue Impact**: Potentially $15-22M campaign generated revenue 
- **Compliance Status**: NON-COMPLIANT with NCC directive 

## Recommendation
Legal challenge against the NCC appears more favorable given:  
- Cost analysis of $50M–$100M vs. compliance costs and estimated losses 
- Compliance with the directive results in significant revenue diminishment.  
Consider preparing legal infrastructure while monitoring the developments allowing for potential future compliance if needed. 

## Risk Flags
- Engaging in a legal battle carries risks of hefty fines and regulatory scrutiny.
- Losing customers during immediate compliance phase could harm brand image and market share.
- Revenue losses from delayed actions may substantially impact quarterly performance targets.  

## Conclusion
Given the potential for significant financial loss through immediate compliance, pursuing a legal challenge while exploring deferment strategies is advisable. Further analysis on the legal pathway and possible negotiations is warranted.
