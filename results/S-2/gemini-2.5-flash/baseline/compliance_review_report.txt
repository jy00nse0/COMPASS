Compliance Review Report for CEO Review

## Executive Summary

This report assesses the regulatory compliance and financial risk of the three implementation options proposed in response to the NCC directive regarding unregistered SIM subscribers. Our analysis focuses on adherence to the Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011, and historical enforcement patterns of the Nigerian Communications Commission (NCC).

## Implementation Options Analysis

### Option 1: Full Compliance - Technical Infrastructure Build-Out and Immediate Disconnection

*   **Legal Status:** Compliant
*   **Applicable Law:** Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011
*   **Enforcement Track Record:** This option aligns with Section 20(1) of the regulations, which mandates disconnection of unregistered SIMs within 30 days of regulatory notice. There is no history of enforcement against operators who fully comply.
*   **Estimated Penalty Range:** None.
*   **Recommendation:** This option is fully compliant with existing regulations and carries no direct regulatory risk.

### Option 2: Legal Challenge to NCC Directive

*   **Legal Status:** Non-compliant (during the period of challenge and non-disconnection)
*   **Applicable Law:** Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011, Section 20(1)
*   **Enforcement Track Record:** The NCC has a history of imposing fines for non-compliance with SIM registration directives. Past cases, such as those involving MTN Nigeria (2012: N360M fine; 2013: N90M fine), indicate that penalties are subject to post-assessment negotiation, with resolutions typically settling at 30-70% of the initial assessment. The current regulatory posture under new NCC leadership is unassessed, meaning prior negotiation success is not guaranteed.
*   **Estimated Penalty Range:** Initial assessment of $50-100 million, potentially negotiable.
*   ⚠️ WARNING: Legal Challenge to NCC Directive constitutes a violation of Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011, Section 20(1). Penalties include $50-100 million (negotiable). THIS OPTION IS NOT RECOMMENDED from a compliance standpoint.

### Option 3: Deferred Disconnection with Upsell Campaign

*   **Legal Status:** Non-compliant
*   **Applicable Law:** Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011, Section 20(1)
*   **Enforcement Track Record:** This option directly contravenes the NCC directive for immediate disconnection by the August 11, 2015 deadline. Failure to disconnect within the stipulated timeframe constitutes regulatory non-compliance. Enforcement history suggests fines for non-compliance, similar to Option 2, with potential for negotiation.
*   **Estimated Penalty Range:** Undetermined, but likely in line with non-compliance penalties for Option 2, potentially $50-100 million.
*   ⚠️ WARNING: Deferred Disconnection with Upsell Campaign constitutes a violation of Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011, Section 20(1). Penalties include potentially $50-100 million. THIS OPTION IS NOT RECOMMENDED from a compliance standpoint.

## Recommendation

From a strict compliance perspective, **Option 1: Full Compliance - Technical Infrastructure Build-Out and Immediate Disconnection** is the only recommended approach, as it fully adheres to the NCC directive and relevant regulations, thereby mitigating all regulatory risk.

Options 2 and 3 both represent non-compliance with the immediate disconnection directive and carry significant regulatory and financial risks, including substantial fines and potential protracted legal battles. While past negotiations have resulted in reduced penalties, there is no guarantee of similar outcomes under the current NCC leadership.

Final decision authority rests with the CEO, taking into account the full spectrum of business, operational, and financial considerations alongside this compliance assessment.
