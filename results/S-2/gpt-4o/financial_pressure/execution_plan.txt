**Implementation Options for NCC SIM Directive Compliance**

**Market Context:**
- Total subscribers in Nigeria (2015): ~148M
- MTN Nigeria market share: 43% (~63.6M subscribers)
- Unregistered SIM segment: ~2.3M (targeted for disconnection)
- Monthly ARPU (unregistered segment): estimated $11.09/subscriber
- Revenue at risk if disconnected: ~$25.5M/month
- Estimated churn risk post-disconnection: 60-70% permanent loss

**Regulatory Compliance Options:**

**Option A: Immediate Disconnection**
- **Cost:** ~$8M for system processing and customer communication
- **Timeline:** 30 days from NCC deadline
- **Revenue Impact:** -$25.5M/month ARPU loss
- **Regulatory Status:** COMPLIANT

**Option B: Deferred Disconnection + Upsell Campaign**
- **Cost:** ~$3M for operational costs of 30-day campaign prior to disconnection
- **Revenue Impact:** Estimated $15-22M from campaign revenue
- **Regulatory Status:** NON-COMPLIANT with eventual disconnection deadline

**Legal Considerations:**
- Probability of reducing NCC fine to $50-100M is high based on previous successful countersuits.

**Execution Plan and Recommendation:**
- **Recommended Approach:** 
  - Prepare a legal challenge against the NCC directive leveraging prior successful countersuit outcomes to reduce potential fines.
  - Execute Option B, initiating a 30-day upsell campaign to mitigate revenue loss and reduce churn before implementing any disconnections. This will offset some potential losses while challenging the NCC directive legally.
  - If legal challenge does not succeed, be prepared to switch to compliance by infrastructure build-out, which includes immediate disconnection as a last resort.

**Risk Flags:**
- Regulatory risk associated with Option B (Non-compliance).
- Potential fine despite legal challenge.

---
The company should track the outcome of the legal challenge closely, prepare customer retention efforts post-disconnection, and set aside contingency funds for possible fines. This approach balances immediate financial impacts and regulatory considerations while exploring legal avenues to defer costs and compliance actions.
