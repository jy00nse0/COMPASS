## Compliance Review Report: NCC SIM Directive Compliance

### Overview:
This report assesses the regulatory compliance and legal risks of proposed execution plans in response to the NCC's directive for SIM registration compliance. Two principal options are evaluated based on regulatory laws and past enforcement actions.

### Execution Options:

**Option A: Immediate Disconnection**
- **Legal Status:** COMPLIANT
- **Applicable Law:** According to the "Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011," Section 20(1), operators are required to disconnect unregistered SIMs within 30 days following regulatory notice.
- **Enforcement History:** No prior penalties since this option adheres to the stipulated requirements.
- **Estimated Penalty Range if Non-Compliant:** Non-applicable since compliance is present.

**Option B: Deferred Disconnection + Upsell Campaign**
- **Legal Status:** NON-COMPLIANT
- **Applicable Law:** Violates Section 20(1) for not disconnecting unregistered SIMs by NCC’s deadline (August 11, 2015).
- **Enforcement Track Record:**
  - Historical fines have been negotiated successfully in past instances (e.g., 2012 and 2013).
  - Settlement reductions achieved between 30-70% of the initial fines.
- **Estimated Penalty Range:** Possible fines initially assessed between $50M-$100M; NCC fines are not fixed and may be negotiated.
- **⚠️ WARNING:** Option B constitutes a violation of the Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011, Section 20(1). Penalties include substantial fines, but historical patterns suggest potential for negotiated settlements. THIS OPTION IS NOT RECOMMENDED from a compliance standpoint, without a guaranteed successful legal challenge.

### Legal and Financial Risk Considerations:
- The NCC has historically shown willingness to negotiate fines post-assessment, however, given the shift in leadership and untested nature of the current enforcement regime, past successes may not predict future outcomes.
- A legal challenge could potentially reduce fines but remains a speculative strategy depending on the current enforcement posture.
- Revenue risk versus regulatory compliance must be weighed carefully. Implementation of immediate disconnection (Option A) absolves regulatory risk at the potential cost of revenue loss and customer churn.
- Deferred disconnection with an upsell (Option B) may offset some revenue losses but risks substantial non-compliance penalties.

### Recommendations:
The choice between immediate compliance (Option A) and deferred compliance with legal contestation (Option B) involves trade-offs between financial and regulatory risks. While Option B may align with short-term financial goals, the risk of non-compliance could entail penalties that outweigh these benefits. Therefore, it is crucial to:
- Closely monitor legal outcomes if pursuing Option B.
- Prepare for customer retention post-disconnection.
- Maintain contingencies for potential NCC-imposed fines.
