## Compliance Review Report

### Assessment of Execution Plan for Legal Challenge Against NCC Directive

#### Context Summary
This report evaluates the execution plan aimed at legally challenging the Nigerian Communications Commission (NCC) directive on SIM registration, prepared by the Strategic Planning Manager. The primary goal is to mitigate financial impacts while addressing compliance risks related to the disconnection of unregistered SIM cards.

### Step-by-Step Compliance and Legal Risk Assessment

#### Step 1: Market and Regulatory Context
- **Option A (Immediate Disconnection)**
  - **Legal Status:** Complies with Sections 19 and 20(1) of the "Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011."
  - **Applicable Legislation:** Fulfillment of NCC's mandatory disconnection directive within the stipulated timeframe.
  - **Recommendation:** Compliance is legally sound though financially adverse. The approach is recommended to avoid NCC penalties, despite significant churn impact.

- **Option B (Deferred Disconnection with Campaign)**
  - **Legal Status:** Non-compliant with the mandatory disconnection directive.
  - **Applicable Legislation:** Violation of NCC's disconnection requirements set forth in Section 20(1).
  - **Enforcement Track Record:** Historically, MTN has encountered fines which were significantly reduced through negotiations (30-70% reduction). However, these reductions cannot be guaranteed under current NCC authorities.
  - ⚠️ **WARNING:** Deferred disconnection constitutes a violation of "Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011" Section 20(1). Penalties are determined by the NCC and historically subject to negotiation, but could still be substantial. THIS OPTION IS NOT RECOMMENDED from a compliance standpoint.

#### Step 2: Legal Challenge Execution Plan
- **Historical Context:**
  - Past engagements show a track record of successfully negotiating down penalties imposed by the NCC.
  - Prior fines of up to N360M were reduced through strategic legal challenges.
- **Current NCC Posture:**
  - Unknown enforcement stance under the current NCC leadership post-shifting guidelines.
  - Legal route presents risk due to lack of current negotiation success predictability.
- **Financial Risk:**
  - Initial legal costs estimated at $2-3M with revenue retention goals.
  - Protracted legal delays could expose MTN to unresolved NCC penalties and tarnish public perception.

### Conclusion and Recommendation
The execution plan proposes significant risks under current regulatory expectations. While foreign legal actions can temporize imminent financial consequences, uncertainty around current NCC leadership's penalty stance exists. Direct compliance, though financially burdensome, guarantees legal safety. The deferred disconnection approach, unsupported by recent successful negotiations, should not be pursued.

Final decision authority remains with the CEO, who may consider broader strategic and financial drivers in making an ultimate decision beyond compliance focal points. Continuous monitoring of NCC directives and enforcement trends is advised to inform future company decisions.
