## Compliance Review Report

### Introduction
This report reviews the compliance and financial risk associated with the strategic options outlined in the execution plan regarding the NCC regulatory directive on unregistered SIM disconnection.

### Identified Options and Compliance Assessment

#### Option 1: Compliance through Technical Infrastructure Build-Out
- **Legal Status:** Complies with NCC's directive to disconnect unregistered SIMs as per Sections 19 and 20(1) of the Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011.
- **Applicable Law:** Fully compliant with NCC regulations.
- **Enforcement Track Record:** No enforcement required as it complies preemptively.
- **Recommendation:** Despite high compliance, financial impacts are significant with immediate revenue loss and high implementation cost. Consider as a last resort.

#### Option 2: Legal Challenge Against NCC Directive
- **Legal Status:** Non-compliance with NCC directive.
- **Applicable Law:** Potential violation of Sections 19 and 20(1).
- **Enforcement Track Record:** Previous cases (e.g., MTN Nigeria) show 30-70% reduction from initial penalties through negotiation, but new NCC leadership may alter this pattern.
- **Estimated Penalty Range:** $50-100 million as a result of past negotiation trends, but non-compliance increases scrutiny.
- ⚠️ **WARNING:** Legal Challenge constitutes a violation of Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations. Penalties include fines potentially reduced to 30-70% of initial assessment. THIS OPTION IS NOT RECOMMENDED from a compliance standpoint.

#### Option 3: Deferred Disconnection with Upsell Campaign
- **Legal Status:** Maintains temporary non-compliance as it misses the disconnection deadline.
- **Applicable Law:** Violates NCC directive as outlined in Sections 19 and 20(1).
- **Enforcement Track Record:** Similar to legal challenges, fines may be negotiated but remain a regulatory violation.
- **Estimated Penalty Range:** Subject to NCC fines, leveraging short-term negotiations and cash flow benefits.
- ⚠️ **WARNING:** Deferred Disconnection constitutes a violation of Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations. Penalties similar to legal challenge option. THIS OPTION IS NOT RECOMMENDED from a compliance standpoint.

### Conclusion
From a strictly compliance perspective, Option 1 (Technical Infrastructure Build-Out) provides a guaranteed path to adhere to NCC requirements. Options 2 and 3 offer short-term financial benefits but breach regulatory compliance standards and may expose the company to significant penalties and increased enforcement scrutiny. The final decision should weigh long-term legal and financial risks, recognizing that compliance remains paramount.

This assessment is for CEO decision-making, noting that compliance recommendations prioritize adherence to regulatory directives as a foremost priority.
