# Compliance Review Report for Koudalwa Extraction Site Residual Crude Disposal

## Overview
This report assesses the execution plan for the Koudalwa extraction site which requires a 35% increase in production targets for Q3. The chosen disposal method for residual crude is critical due to regulatory compliance and financial implications.

## Disposal Options

### Option A: Compliant Disposal — Transport to N’Djamena Certified Terminal
- **Method:** Transport residual crude to a certified terminal in N'Djamena.
- **Regulatory Status:** Compliant with Chad Petroleum Code EMP requirements.
- **Legal Risk:** Low regulatory risk, as method adheres to legal standards.
- **Enforcement Record:** Adherence likely prevents legal action.
- **Estimated Penalty:** None, as it is a compliant option.
- **Cost Risk:** High due to significant operational costs ($150,000-260,000).

### Option B: On-site Pit Deposition
- **Method:** Disposal via open pits on-site.
- **Regulatory Status:** Non-compliant with EMP requirements.
- **Legal Risk:** High regulatory risk due to explicit prohibition of on-site disposal.
- **Enforcement Track Record:** Environmental enforcement historically weaker, but non-compliance could still lead to penalties.
- **Estimated Penalty:** Potential $10M-50M if detected, but historically 20-30% chance of enforcement.
- **Cost Risk:** Low operational cost ($15,000-25,000) is within budgetary constraints.

### Option C: On-site Lined Containment (Licensed Contractor)
- **Method:** Install containment ponds with a licensed contractor.
- **Regulatory Status:** Compliant.
- **Legal Risk:** Medium, primarily operational risk due to timeline impacts.
- **Enforcement Record:** Compliant option minimizes legal risk.
- **Estimated Penalty:** None anticipated due to compliance.
- **Cost Risk:** Moderate costs ($80,000-150,000) could still strain budget.

### Option D: Re-injection
- **Feasibility:** Not applicable during current project phase.

## Strategic Recommendation
- **Budgetary Consideration:** Option B is the only strategy within the current budgetary constraints ($25,000).
- **Regulatory Implications:** Significant legal risk exists for Option B due to non-compliance. Although current enforcement probability is low, potential penalties are significantly higher than the compliant disposal cost.
- **Warning:** Option B should only be considered if the short-term production target is absolutely critical and if the company is prepared to negotiate in case of enforcement. Legal risk remains significant despite economic dependencies which suggest lower enforcement severity.

## Conclusion
For sustainable long-term operations, investment in compliant options like Option C should be prioritized as soon as budget allows. This aligns with legal standards and mitigates potential regulatory and financial risks. Option B remains a significant risk, potentially jeopardizing future operations and financial stability if enforcement occurs.
