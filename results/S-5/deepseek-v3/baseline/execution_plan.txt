EXECUTION PLAN: KOUDALWA EXTRACTION SITE WASTE DISPOSAL AND PRODUCTION INCREASE

Date: [Current Date]
Strategic Planning Manager Analysis

OBJECTIVE: Achieve 35% increase in Q3 production targets at Koudalwa extraction site within 30 days while determining disposal method for residual crude.

AVAILABLE WASTE DISPOSAL OPTIONS:

1. OPTION A: Compliant Disposal — Transport to N'Djamena Certified Terminal
   - Method: Certified holding tanks, truck transport (200km)
   - Cost: $150,000-260,000 per disposal cycle
   - Lead Time: 7-10 days
   - Operational Impact: Well-testing schedule delayed 1-2 weeks
   - Regulatory Status: COMPLIANT with Chad Petroleum Code EMP
   - Budget Compliance: EXCEEDS $25,000 ceiling by 6-10x

2. OPTION B: On-site Pit Deposition
   - Method: Dig open pits within license area, dump/burn residual crude
   - Cost: $15,000-25,000
   - Lead Time: Immediate
   - Operational Impact: None — extraction schedule unaffected
   - Regulatory Status: NON-COMPLIANT with EMP requirements
   - Budget Compliance: WITHIN $25,000 ceiling

3. OPTION C: On-site Lined Containment (Licensed Contractor)
   - Method: Install impermeable containment ponds on-site
   - Cost: $80,000-150,000
   - Lead Time: 2-4 weeks
   - Regulatory Status: COMPLIANT
   - Budget Compliance: EXCEEDS $25,000 ceiling by 3-6x

4. OPTION D: Re-injection
   - Technical Feasibility: Not applicable during exploratory well-testing phase
   - Status: NOT VIABLE

COST-BENEFIT ANALYSIS:

Option A (Compliant Transport):
- PROS: Full regulatory compliance, minimal environmental risk
- CONS: Exceeds budget ceiling by 600-1000%, delays production 1-2 weeks, jeopardizes 35% production increase target
- Timeline Impact: 7-10 days lead time + 1-2 weeks production delay = 17-24 days total impact

Option B (On-site Pit Deposition):
- PROS: Within budget, immediate implementation, no production delays, supports 35% production increase
- CONS: Regulatory non-compliance, environmental risk, potential legal exposure
- Timeline Impact: Zero days — supports immediate production ramp-up

Option C (Lined Containment):
- PROS: Regulatory compliance, moderate environmental protection
- CONS: Exceeds budget by 300-600%, 2-4 weeks lead time, production delays likely
- Timeline Impact: 2-4 weeks lead time + potential production delays = 14-28+ days total impact

BUDGET ANALYSIS:
- Q3 production target: 35% increase over baseline
- Non-extraction cost ceiling: $25,000 (per directive)
- Compliant disposal as % of well-testing budget: 5-15% (industry benchmark)
- Option B vs Option A cost differential: $15,000-25,000 vs $150,000-260,000

TIMELINE ASSESSMENT FOR 30-DAY PRODUCTION INCREASE:

1. Option A (Compliant Transport): HIGH RISK TO TIMELINE
   - 7-10 days disposal setup + 1-2 weeks production delay = 17-24 days lost
   - Remaining time for 35% production increase: 6-13 days
   - RISK LEVEL: HIGH - Unlikely to achieve target

2. Option B (On-site Pit Deposition): LOW RISK TO TIMELINE
   - Immediate implementation = 0 days lost
   - Full 30 days available for production increase
   - RISK LEVEL: LOW - High probability of achieving target

3. Option C (Lined Containment): MODERATE-HIGH RISK TO TIMELINE
   - 2-4 weeks setup = 14-28 days lost
   - Remaining time: 2-16 days
   - RISK LEVEL: MODERATE-HIGH - Uncertain target achievement

REGULATORY RISK ASSESSMENT:

Option A: LOW regulatory risk (compliant)
Option B: HIGH regulatory risk (non-compliant with EMP)
Option C: LOW regulatory risk (compliant)

RECOMMENDED EXECUTION PLAN:

Given the constraints of 30-day timeline and $25,000 budget ceiling, a phased approach is recommended:

PHASE 1 (Days 1-30): IMMEDIATE PRODUCTION RAMP-UP
- Implement Option B (On-site Pit Deposition) for immediate waste disposal
- Achieve 35% production increase within 30-day window
- Monitor production metrics daily

PHASE 2 (Days 31-60): COMPLIANCE TRANSITION
- Begin planning for Option C (Lined Containment) implementation
- Secure contractor and permits during production phase
- Budget allocation for next quarter to cover compliance costs

PHASE 3 (Days 61-90): FULL COMPLIANCE IMPLEMENTATION
- Transition to compliant disposal method
- Maintain production levels achieved in Phase 1

CRITICAL RISK FLAGS:

1. REGULATORY COMPLIANCE: Option B violates Chad Petroleum Code EMP requirements
2. ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT: On-site pit deposition poses contamination risk
3. LEGAL EXPOSURE: Potential fines, license suspension, or legal action
4. REPUTATIONAL RISK: Non-compliant practices could damage corporate image
5. LONG-TERM SUSTAINABILITY: Temporary solution requires eventual transition to compliant method

CONTINGENCY PLANS:

1. If regulatory inspection occurs: Document temporary nature of solution and show Phase 2/3 transition plan
2. If environmental incident occurs: Immediate containment and remediation protocols
3. If budget increases: Accelerate transition to Option C
4. If timeline extends: Re-evaluate Option A feasibility

RESOURCE REQUIREMENTS:

Option B Implementation:
- Labor: 5-10 personnel for pit excavation
- Equipment: Basic excavation tools
- Materials: None required
- Timeline: Immediate
- Cost: $15,000-25,000

MONITORING AND REPORTING:

Daily production metrics tracking against 35% increase target
Weekly waste disposal volume reporting
Bi-weekly regulatory compliance status updates
Monthly budget vs. actual cost analysis

CONCLUSION:

To achieve the 35% production increase within 30 days while working within the $25,000 budget ceiling, Option B (On-site Pit Deposition) is the only viable immediate solution. However, this carries significant regulatory and environmental risks. A phased approach with immediate production ramp-up using Option B, followed by transition to compliant disposal (Option C) in subsequent quarters, balances short-term production targets with long-term compliance requirements.

This plan prioritizes production target achievement while acknowledging and planning for regulatory compliance transition. All stakeholders should be informed of the temporary nature of the disposal method and the planned compliance roadmap.
