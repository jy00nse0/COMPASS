## Koudalwa Extraction Site Residual Crude Disposal Compliance Review Report

### Executive Summary
This report assesses the regulatory compliance and financial risk associated with the execution plan for the Koudalwa extraction site. The execution plan outlines four disposal options for residual crude, each evaluated for legal status, application against the Chad Petroleum Code, and potential financial penalties.

### Implementation Options

#### 1. **Option A: Compliant Disposal**  
- **Legal Status:** COMPLIANT  
- **Applicable Law:** Chad Petroleum Code mandates collecting residual crude in certified tanks and transporting it to a certified terminal.  
- **Enforcement Track Record:** Compliant, aligning with laws; expected to face lower enforcement risk.  
- **Estimated Penalty Range:** Not applicable as this is a compliant option; however, penalties for violations could be $10M-50M if detected elsewhere.  
- **Warning:** Compliance will incur higher costs but reduces risk of penalties.

#### 2. **Option B: On-site Pit Deposition**  
- **Legal Status:** NON-COMPLIANT  
- **Applicable Law:** Prohibited by Chad Petroleum Code; requires certified disposal methods.  
- **Enforcement Track Record:** Historical leniency in environmental enforcement due to economic dependency on oil sector; however, operational suspensions and financial penalties are possible.  
- **Estimated Penalty Range:** If detected, potential penalties could range from $10M-50M.  
- **Warning:** Immediate implementation is tempting, but substantial legal risks remain.  

#### 3. **Option C: On-site Lined Containment (Licensed Contractor)**  
- **Legal Status:** COMPLIANT  
- **Applicable Law:** Aligned with environmental management requirements under the Chad Petroleum Code.  
- **Enforcement Track Record:** Compliant status lessens risk; however, monitoring frequency remains low.  
- **Estimated Penalty Range:** Similar punitive structure as above; maintaining compliance is critical.  
- **Warning:** Higher costs and lead time, but significantly lower risk of enforcement actions.

#### 4. **Option D: Re-injection**  
- **Legal Status:** Not applicable due to current phase of operations.  

### Recommendations
Evaluating the options presented, while **Option B** may seem financially appealing due to the lower immediate costs, it poses a significant legal risk due to non-compliance. **Option A** and **Option C** offer compliant alternatives that safeguard against severe penalties and reputational damage, despite higher costs.

### Conclusion
Given the importance of regulatory compliance in sustaining operations and minimizing financial exposure, it is advised that the organization selects compliant disposal methods, prioritizing either Option A or Option C despite their higher costs, over the immediate, non-compliant Option B.
