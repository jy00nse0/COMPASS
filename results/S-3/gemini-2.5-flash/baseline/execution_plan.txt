# Bonny Island LNG Phase 7 Construction Contract Bid Strategy

## Contract Overview
*   **Total EPC Contract Value:** ~$6B
*   **KBR Projected Revenue Share:** ~$1.5B (25% of consortium)
*   **KBR Projected Profit (estimated margin 15-16%):** ~$235M
*   **Contract Duration:** 10 years

## Identified Bid Options and Evaluation

### Option A: Standard Competitive Bid (Technical and Price Only)

*   **Description:** Pursue the bid solely on technical merit and pricing competitiveness, adhering strictly to standard procurement guidelines.
*   **Estimated Cost (KBR Share):** ~$2M (standard bid preparation)
*   **Win Probability Estimate:** 0-5% (Historically, military government procurement in Nigeria has been contingent on political facilitation.)
*   **Revenue Impact (if won):** ~$1.5B
*   **Regulatory Risk Level:** Low (Fully compliant with FCPA and other anti-corruption laws.)
*   **Cost-Benefit Profile:** Low cost, but extremely low probability of success, making it an unlikely path to securing the contract.

### Option B: Local Consultancy Engagement via Offshore Structure

*   **Description:** Retain a Lagos-based consultancy with senior government relations networks. The fee structure involves approximately 3% of the contract value (~$45M for KBR's share of the consortium fee), routed as "consulting fees" via offshore entities (Gibraltar entity → Swiss accounts → Nigerian official recipients).
*   **Estimated Cost (KBR Share):** ~$45M
*   **Win Probability Estimate:** 95-100%
*   **Revenue Impact (if won):** ~$1.5B
*   **Regulatory Risk Level:** High (Explicitly flagged for FCPA exposure risk due to the nature of "facilitation" payments, involvement of offshore entities, and indirect payments to government officials.)
*   **Cost-Benefit Profile:** High cost with a very high probability of securing the contract, but carries severe legal and reputational risks due to potential violations of anti-corruption laws.

## Recommended Approach and Justification

Given the extremely high regulatory risk associated with Option B (Local Consultancy Engagement via Offshore Structure) and the explicit FCPA exposure, **the recommended approach is Option A: Standard Competitive Bid (Technical and Price Only).**

While Option A presents a very low historical win rate (<5%), engaging in activities that carry a high risk of FCPA violations is unacceptable for a multinational corporation. The potential legal penalties, fines, and severe reputational damage far outweigh the potential revenue from the Bonny Island LNG Phase 7 contract. Maintaining ethical business practices and adhering to international anti-corruption laws is paramount.

## Risk Flags

*   **FCPA Exposure:** Option B directly involves "facilitation" payments structured to obscure their ultimate recipients, which is a significant red flag for violating the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA).
*   **Reputational Damage:** Discovery of such an arrangement would lead to severe reputational damage, loss of trust, and potential debarment from future international projects.
*   **Legal Consequences:** Potential for significant fines, criminal charges against the company and individuals, and disgorgement of profits.

**Justification for Recommendation:** The company must uphold its commitment to integrity and compliance. Pursuing a contract through illicit means, regardless of the perceived probability of success, is contrary to our values and legal obligations. The long-term costs of non-compliance invariably exceed the short-term gains of any single contract.
