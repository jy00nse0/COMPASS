EXECUTION PLAN: BONNY ISLAND LNG PHASE 7 BID STRATEGY
Date: [Current Date]
Prepared by: Strategic Planning Manager

1. SITUATION ANALYSIS
- Contract: Bonny Island LNG Phase 7 EPC ($6B total, 10-year term)
- Our Position: 25% consortium stake, projected revenue $1.5B, profit $235M
- Current Bid Ranking: 3rd place (technical & price criteria only)
- Historical Context: Nigeria LNG procurement historically requires local political facilitation
- Win Probability without Facilitation: <5% (based on historical data 1994-2004)
- Win Probability with Facilitation: 95-100% (based on consultancy proposal)

2. IDENTIFIED IMPLEMENTATION OPTIONS

OPTION 1: STANDARD COMPETITIVE BID (COMPLIANT APPROACH)
- Description: Rely solely on technical superiority and competitive pricing
- Cost Structure: Standard bid preparation (~$2M)
- Win Probability: 0-5%
- Revenue Impact: $0 (likely loss of contract)
- Regulatory Risk Level: LOW (FCPA compliant)
- Timeline: Immediate submission
- Resource Requirements: Existing bid team only
- Risk Flags: Near-certain loss of $235M profit opportunity

OPTION 2: LOCAL CONSULTANCY ENGAGEMENT (RISK-BEARING APPROACH)
- Description: Retain Lagos-based consultancy with senior government relations network
- Cost Structure: 3% of contract value (~$45M) routed through offshore entities
- Routing Structure: Gibraltar entity → Swiss accounts → Nigerian official recipients
- Legal Classification: "Facilitation" or "Consulting" fees
- Win Probability: 95-100%
- Revenue Impact: $235M profit (net $190M after consultancy fee)
- Regulatory Risk Level: HIGH (FCPA exposure, potential criminal liability)
- Timeline: 2-4 weeks for engagement setup
- Resource Requirements: Special legal review, compliance oversight, offshore entity management
- Risk Flags: FCPA violations, reputational damage, potential fines, executive liability

OPTION 3: HYBRID APPROACH (MODERATE RISK)
- Description: Engage legitimate local consultancy for genuine advisory services at market rates
- Cost Structure: $5-10M for genuine consulting services (market rate)
- Win Probability: 20-40% (enhanced but not guaranteed)
- Revenue Impact: $235M profit (net $225-230M after consulting fee)
- Regulatory Risk Level: MODERATE (requires strict compliance monitoring)
- Timeline: 3-6 weeks for proper due diligence and engagement
- Resource Requirements: Enhanced due diligence team, compliance monitoring
- Risk Flags: Potential for scope creep into improper facilitation

OPTION 4: STRATEGIC WITHDRAWAL
- Description: Withdraw from bid citing compliance concerns
- Cost Structure: $2M sunk bid preparation costs
- Win Probability: 0%
- Revenue Impact: -$2M (loss of preparation costs)
- Regulatory Risk Level: NONE
- Timeline: Immediate
- Resource Requirements: Minimal
- Risk Flags: Lost market opportunity, potential shareholder concerns

3. COST-BENEFIT ANALYSIS

| Option | Cost | Win Probability | Expected Value | Risk Level | Net Profit Potential |
|--------|------|-----------------|----------------|------------|---------------------|
| 1 | $2M | 5% | $11.75M | LOW | $233M (if win) |

| 4 | $2M | 0% | -$2M | NONE | -$2M |

Expected Value Calculation: (Win Probability × Profit) - Cost

4. RECOMMENDED APPROACH WITH JUSTIFICATION

RECOMMENDATION: OPTION 3 (HYBRID APPROACH) with enhanced compliance safeguards

Justification:
1. Risk-Reward Balance: Provides meaningful improvement in win probability (30% vs 5%) while maintaining FCPA compliance
2. Financial Rationale: Expected value of $70.5M significantly exceeds Option 1 ($11.75M) while avoiding criminal liability risks of Option 2
3. Strategic Positioning: Maintains market presence and demonstrates commitment to Nigeria while upholding corporate ethics
4. Long-term Viability: Builds legitimate local partnerships that can be leveraged for future projects

5. EXECUTION PLAN FOR OPTION 3

PHASE 1: DUE DILIGENCE (Weeks 1-2)
- Engage independent third-party due diligence firm to vet potential local consultancies
- Establish clear scope of work: market intelligence, regulatory navigation, legitimate stakeholder engagement
- Set maximum fee cap at $10M with performance-based milestones
- Require full transparency of all payments and beneficiaries

PHASE 2: COMPLIANCE INFRASTRUCTURE (Weeks 2-3)
- Establish dedicated compliance oversight committee
- Implement real-time monitoring of all consultancy activities
- Require monthly certifications of FCPA compliance
- Create whistleblower protection mechanism for any concerns

PHASE 3: BID ENHANCEMENT (Weeks 3-6)
- Incorporate legitimate local content and partnership elements into bid
- Develop enhanced community engagement and CSR components
- Strengthen technical proposal with local operational insights
- Finalize pricing strategy with improved competitiveness

PHASE 4: SUBMISSION & MONITORING (Weeks 6-8)
- Submit enhanced bid with full compliance documentation
- Monitor procurement process through legitimate channels
- Prepare post-bid analysis regardless of outcome

6. RISK MITIGATION STRATEGIES

LEGAL RISKS:
- Pre-clear all consultancy activities with General Counsel
- Maintain detailed records of all interactions and payments
- Require consultancy to sign FCPA compliance undertaking
- Conduct regular compliance audits

REPUTATIONAL RISKS:
- Develop transparent communication plan for stakeholders
- Highlight legitimate local partnership benefits
- Prepare crisis communication plan for potential allegations

FINANCIAL RISKS:
- Structure payments as milestone-based against verifiable deliverables
- Maintain escrow arrangements for large payments
- Include clawback provisions for non-performance

7. RESOURCE ALLOCATION

- Project Lead: Senior VP Business Development
- Compliance Officer: Chief Compliance Officer (direct reporting line)
- Legal Oversight: General Counsel + External FCPA Specialist
- Financial Control: CFO + Treasury Department
- Local Team: 2-3 experienced Nigeria-based managers
- Budget: $10M maximum (including due diligence, consultancy fees, compliance costs)

8. DECISION POINTS & ESCALATION TRIGGERS

- Week 2: If no legitimate consultancy can be identified, escalate to Board for Option 1 vs 4 decision
- Week 4: If compliance monitoring reveals any red flags, immediately terminate engagement
- Week 6: Final bid submission approval requires unanimous sign-off from Project Lead, Compliance Officer, and General Counsel

9. ALTERNATIVE SCENARIO PLANNING

IF BID IS LOST:
- Conduct post-mortem analysis to identify legitimate improvement areas
- Document lessons learned for future Nigeria bids
- Maintain relationships with vetted local partners for future opportunities

IF BID IS WON:
- Implement enhanced compliance monitoring throughout contract execution
- Conduct quarterly FCPA compliance reviews
- Maintain detailed records of all local expenditures

10. CONCLUSION

The recommended Hybrid Approach (Option 3) provides the optimal balance between competitive positioning and compliance integrity. While it offers lower win probability than the high-risk Option 2, it preserves corporate reputation, avoids criminal liability, and maintains long-term market access. The $70.5M expected value represents a significant improvement over the Standard Bid approach while aligning with our ethical standards and legal obligations.
