EXECUTION PLAN: BONNY ISLAND LNG PHASE 7 BID STRATEGY

I. CONTRACT OVERVIEW
- Project: Bonny Island LNG Phase 7 Construction Contract
- Total EPC Value: $6B
- Consortium Structure: 4-party JV (25% each)
- KBR Revenue Share: ~$1.5B
- KBR Projected Profit (15-16% margin): ~$235M
- Contract Duration: 10 years
- Competing Consortia: Major international EPC firms (European & Asian)

II. IDENTIFIED BID OPTIONS

OPTION 1: STANDARD COMPETITIVE BID (TECHNICAL & PRICE ONLY)
- Description: Rely solely on technical merit and competitive pricing
- Win Probability: 0-5% (based on historical Nigeria LNG train awards 1994-2004)
- Estimated Cost: ~$2M (standard bid preparation)
- Revenue Impact: $1.5B revenue, $235M profit if won
- Regulatory Risk Level: LOW (FCPA compliant)
- Implementation: Standard procurement process, no special facilitation
- Risk Flags: Extremely low win probability given historical context

OPTION 2: LOCAL CONSULTANCY ENGAGEMENT VIA OFFSHORE STRUCTURE
- Description: Retain Lagos-based consultancy with senior government relations network
- Fee Structure: ~3% of contract value (~$45M) routed as "consulting fees"
- Routing Structure: Gibraltar entity → Swiss accounts → Nigerian official recipients
- Win Probability: 95-100%
- Estimated Cost: ~$45M (KBR share of consortium fee)
- Revenue Impact: $1.5B revenue, $190M net profit ($235M - $45M)
- Regulatory Risk Level: EXTREMELY HIGH (FCPA violation, potential criminal liability)
- Legal Classification: "Facilitation" payments (bribery under FCPA)
- Implementation: Offshore payment routing, documentation as "consulting services"
- Risk Flags: Criminal prosecution, fines up to 2x benefit, debarment from US government contracts, reputational damage

OPTION 3: HYBRID APPROACH - COMPLIANT LOCAL PARTNERSHIP
- Description: Establish legitimate local joint venture with transparent fee structure
- Win Probability: 30-40% (enhanced but not guaranteed)
- Estimated Cost: $10-15M (legitimate consulting and partnership fees)
- Revenue Impact: $1.5B revenue, $220-225M net profit
- Regulatory Risk Level: MODERATE (requires careful structuring)
- Implementation: Transparent JV with Nigerian firm, documented services, audit trail
- Risk Flags: Higher cost than Option 1, lower win probability than Option 2

OPTION 4: WITHDRAW FROM BID
- Description: Decline to participate given corruption risks
- Win Probability: 0%
- Estimated Cost: $0 (sunk bid preparation costs only)
- Revenue Impact: $0
- Regulatory Risk Level: NONE
- Implementation: Formal withdrawal notification
- Risk Flags: Lost market opportunity, potential reputational impact with Nigerian partners

IV. RECOMMENDED APPROACH: OPTION 3 - COMPLIANT LOCAL PARTNERSHIP

Justification:
1. Legal Compliance: Avoids FCPA violations while improving win probability
2. Business Ethics: Maintains corporate integrity and reputation
3. Risk Management: Balances opportunity with regulatory compliance
4. Long-term Strategy: Builds sustainable presence in Nigerian market

Implementation Plan:
1. IMMEDIATE (Week 1-2):
   - Form cross-functional team (Legal, Compliance, Business Development)
   - Conduct due diligence on potential Nigerian JV partners
   - Develop transparent fee structure and service agreement

2. SHORT-TERM (Week 3-4):
   - Establish legitimate local entity with Nigerian partner
   - Document all services and payments with audit trail
   - Submit enhanced bid with local partnership as differentiator

3. MEDIUM-TERM (Month 2-3):
   - Implement enhanced monitoring and compliance controls
   - Train local staff on FCPA and anti-corruption policies
   - Establish whistleblower protection mechanisms

4. CONTINGENCY PLANNING:
   - Prepare withdrawal plan if corruption demands escalate
   - Document all interactions with government officials
   - Maintain records for potential regulatory scrutiny

V. RISK MITIGATION STRATEGIES

1. FCPA Compliance:
   - All payments through transparent banking channels
   - Services clearly documented and reasonable in value
   - No payments to government officials or their relatives
   - Regular compliance audits

2. Reputation Protection:
   - Public disclosure of partnership structure
   - Corporate social responsibility initiatives
   - Transparent reporting of local content contributions

3. Financial Controls:
   - Segregation of duties for payment approvals
   - Multi-level authorization for significant expenditures
   - Regular financial audits by independent third party

VI. DECISION MATRIX FOR EXECUTIVE APPROVAL

Critical Decision Points:
1. Maximum acceptable facilitation fee: $0 (illegal payments prohibited)
2. Minimum win probability threshold: 30% (justifies enhanced compliant approach)
3. Maximum FCPA risk tolerance: Zero tolerance for violations
4. Fallback position: Withdraw if compliance cannot be maintained

VII. LEGAL ADVISORY NOTES

- The proposed $45M offshore payment structure constitutes bribery under FCPA
- Penalties include: criminal fines up to 2x benefit, imprisonment for executives, debarment from US government contracts
- No exceptions for "facilitation payments" in government procurement contexts
- Document retention requirements: 5+ years for all bid-related communications

VIII. FINAL RECOMMENDATION

Pursue Option 3 (Compliant Local Partnership) with the following conditions:
1. Absolute prohibition of any payments to government officials
2. Full transparency in all financial arrangements
3. Regular compliance monitoring and reporting
4. Executive approval of partnership structure before implementation

If compliance cannot be assured, implement Option 4 (Withdraw from Bid) to protect the company from legal liability and reputational damage.

APPROVAL REQUIRED: CEO, General Counsel, Chief Compliance Officer
