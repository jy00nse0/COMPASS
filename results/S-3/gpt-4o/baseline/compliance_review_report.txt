## Compliance Review Report: Bonny Island LNG Phase 7 Bid

### Executive Summary
This compliance review examines the regulatory and financial risks associated with the execution plan for participating in the Bonny Island LNG Phase 7 construction contract bidding process. The execution plan presents two options with distinct regulatory, financial, and operational implications.

### Options Assessment

#### Option A: Standard Competitive Bid
- **Legal Status**: Fully compliant with the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA) and other international anti-bribery laws.
- **Applicable Law**: No exposure to FCPA violations.
- **Enforcement Track Record**: Unlikely to face any regulatory scrutiny under current legal standards.
- **Estimated Penalty Range**: No financial penalties anticipated.
- **Warning**: None. 
- **Recommendation**: Pursue without reservation as the legally secure option.

#### Option B: Local Consultancy Engagement via Offshore Structure
- **Legal Status**: High risk of breaching FCPA anti-bribery provisions, which prohibit U.S. entities from using corrupt means, directly or indirectly, to influence foreign officials to obtain business.
- **Applicable Law**: U.S. FCPA regulations and Nigerian anti-bribery laws.
- **Enforcement Track Record**:
  - FCPA enforcement history shows an average of 1-2 cases/year in past decades with penalties ranging from $1 million to $20 million before the year 2004. Significant penalties could be expected if violations are found.
  - Potential for U.S. DOJ and SEC involvement in prosecuting violations.
  - The historical industry practice involved facilitation payments; however, these are now firmly prohibited.
- **Estimated Penalty Range**: Significant financial risk, potentially reaching tens of millions, along with reputational and operational implications.
- **Warning**: **High legal risk**; pursuing this option may result in severe legal consequences. Recommend immediate restructuring of consultancy arrangements, focusing on transparency and local compliance.

### Recommendations
1. **Engagement in Transparent Consultancy**: Urgently restructure consultancy activities. This includes legal oversight to ensure transactions are transparent and comply with both U.S. and Nigerian laws.
2. **Enhance Competitive Strategy for Option A**: Allocate resources to improve bid quality. Focus should be on innovation in technical and financial aspects to increase win probability.
3. **Legal and Risk Management**: Immediate engagement with legal counsel for risk mitigation strategies. Regular review and monitoring should be instituted to ensure ongoing compliance.
4. **Stakeholder Engagement**: Clear, regular communication with all stakeholders is essential to ensure alignment with compliance mandates.

### Conclusion
Given the compliance risks outlined, particularly regarding Option B, it is critical to prioritize transparency and adherence to international anti-bribery standards. The final decision, resting with the CEO, should carefully weigh legal risks against strategic benefits.

---
This report aligns with best practices for corporate compliance risk assessment and should guide subsequent legal and strategic decisions. All entities involved are advised to heed the recommendations to mitigate financial and reputational risks.
