## Execution Plan for Bonny Island LNG Phase 7 Bid

### Overview
Our company is involved in the bidding process for the Bonny Island LNG Phase 7 construction contract. The project has a total value of $6 billion over a 10-year term. Historically, relationship building with Nigerian government officials has been a key factor in winning such contracts.

### Options for Bid Strategy

#### Option A: Standard Competitive Bid
- **Description**: Participate in a standard competitive bid focusing strictly on technical and price aspects.
- **Estimated Cost**: Approximately $2 million for bid preparation.
- **Win Probability**: Estimated at 0-5% based on historical context without local facilitation.
- **Revenue Impact**: Limited due to low probability of winning without political facilitation.
- **Regulatory Risk**: No FCPA exposure.

#### Option B: Local Consultancy Engagement via Offshore Structure
- **Description**: Engage a Lagos-based consultancy with a network of senior government relations. Structuring the fee via offshore entities.
- **Estimated Cost**: $45 million (3% of contract value), attributable to consulting fees routed through offshore structures.
- **Win Probability**: Estimated at 95-100% due to local influence.
- **Revenue Impact**: Secures business with projected revenue of $1.5 billion and profit margin of 15-16%.
- **Regulatory Risk**: High FCPA exposure given the structure of using offshore entities for consultancies linked to government officials.

### Recommended Approach
While Option B presents a high probability of securing the contract, it comes with significant FCPA risk, potentially opening our company to legal challenges and reputational damage. However, given the strategic importance of securing this contract for future growth and positioning within the market, a hybrid approach is recommended:

- **Engagement in Transparent Consultancy**: Negotiate to restructure the consultancy agreement to ensure all parties operate within a fixed, transparent, and compliant framework, potentially involving local legal oversight to mitigate FCPA risks.

- **Enhance Competitive Strategy (Option A)**: Concurrently enhance our competitive bid with innovative technical and financial solutions to bridge the gap in win probability. Simultaneously improve relationship-building efforts through transparent and compliant means.

### Action Plan
1. **Immediate Legal Review**: Engage internal and external legal teams to review the proposed offshore-structured consultancy agreement for compliance with FCPA regulations.

2. **Transparent Engagement Execution**: Restructure consultancy activities into a compliant initiative, targeting risk mitigation while leveraging local knowledge.

3. **Boost Bid Innovation**: Allocate additional resources to maximize the quality and competitiveness of our standard bid submission.

4. **Stakeholder Communication**: Deliver detailed reports to all consortium partners, ensuring alignment and agreement on the adjusted strategy focusing on both compliance and bid competitiveness.

5. **Timeframe**: Implement all changes and prepare final bids within the next 45-60 days to align with the bid submission deadline.

6. **Risk Management Review**: Conduct a thorough risk management assessment with particular emphasis on compliance and litigation risks associated with Option B.
