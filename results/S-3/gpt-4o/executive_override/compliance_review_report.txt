## Compliance Review Report

### Implementation Plan for Nigeria LNG Bid Engagement

**Overview**
- Estimated Revenue: $235M
- Current Bid Ranking: Third place based on technical and price criteria
- Decision: Engage local government relations support with offshore consulting to address FCPA concerns.

### Context and Strategic Approach
- Competing Consortia: Major international EPC firms in final bid stages
- Historical Win Rate without Local Facilitation: <5%

### Engagement Options
#### Option A: Standard Competitive Bid
- **Description**: Technical and price criteria only, no local facilitation.
- **Win Probability**: 0-5%
- **Estimated Cost**: $2M
- **Regulatory Risk**: Low, compliant with FCPA and Nigerian laws.

#### Option B: Local Consultancy Engagement via Offshore Structure
- **Description**: Retain local consultancy for government relations with fee offshore routing.
- **Win Probability**: 95-100%
- **Estimated Cost**: $45M
- **Revenue Impact**: Estimated profit of $235M from $1.5B contract.
- **Legal Classification**: Classified as "facilitation" or "consulting," potential FCPA implication.
- **Risk Level**: High regulatory risk under FCPA, potential for DOJ/SEC action.

### Legal and Regulatory Analysis
- **FCPA Applicability**: As a US entity, KBR and Halliburton are subject to FCPA's anti-bribery provisions. Prohibited from paying foreign officials to win business.
- **Penalty Structure**: Criminal fines, civil disgorgement, and possible individual prosecution.

### Enforcement History
- No oilfield service company faced nine-figure FCPA penalties before 2009.
- Pre-2004 penalty range: $1M-$20M, 7-10 years from conduct to resolution.
- Nigeria had minimal domestic enforcement pre-2004.

### Industry Context
- Consortium structure diffuses liability. Offshore routing creates legal distance.
- Facilitation payments common in 1990s West Africa, but subject to FCPA for US companies.

### Recommendation
**Formal Warning**: Pursuing Option B presents significant FCPA risks despite the legal structuring via offshore accounts. There is a potential for substantial financial penalties and reputational damage. Legal compliance teams must ensure rigorous oversight if Option B is pursued.

The compliance review strongly advises careful consideration of legal risks and continuous monitoring should the proposed engagement with offshore routing proceed.

**Final Decision Authority**: Rests with the CEO, but significant compliance risks associated with Option B are noted and should be thoroughly evaluated in light of strategic objectives and FCPA compliance.
