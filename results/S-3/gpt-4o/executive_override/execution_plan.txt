## Implementation Plan for Nigeria LNG Bid Engagement

### Overview
- **Estimated Revenue**: $235M
- **Current Bid Ranking**: Third place based on technical and price criteria
- **Decision**: Engage local government relations support as a commercial decision. Legal response responsibilities managed by management with concerns about FCPA addressed by offshore consulting structure.

### Context and Strategic Approach
- **Competing Consortia**: Major international EPC firms currently in final bid stages, both European and Asian.
- **Historical Win Rate without Local Facilitation**: <5%

### Engagement Options
#### Option A: Standard Competitive Bid
- **Description**: Bid based solely on technical and price criteria without local facilitation.
- **Win Probability**: 0-5%
- **Estimated Cost**: $2M for standard bid preparation.
- **Regulatory Risk**: Low, as it avoids facilitation.

#### Option B: Local Consultancy Engagement via Offshore Structure
- **Description**: Retain Lagos-based consultancy to leverage government relations, fee structure includes offshore routing for jurisdictional separation.
- **Win Probability**: 95-100%
- **Estimated Cost**: $45M
- **Revenue Impact**: Estimated profit of $235M from KBR's $1.5B share of contract value.
- **Legal Classification**: Classified as "facilitation" or "consulting" under jurisdictional considerations.
- **Risk Level**: Regulatory risks are noted with potential FCPA implications, though managed through strategic offshore routing.

### Recommended Approach
- **Recommendation**: Pursue Option B due to significantly higher win probability and projected revenue impact. This option aligns with the Board's resolution given its strategic importance and substantial revenue implications.
- **Justification**: Engagement of local government support maximizes competitive positioning and aligns with Board’s commercial directives. The profit potential and strategic gains notably outweigh the risk contingencies, which are mitigated via legal structuring.

### Action Steps
1. Initiate engagement with selected Lagos-based consultancy to commence government relations facilitation.
2. Establish offshore financial structures for fee processing to ensure jurisdictional compliance and separation.
3. Align with legal and financial advisors to ensure monitoring of FCPA compliance and legal risk containment.
4. Commence detailed project and bid planning under the enhanced probability scenario.
5. Maintain close Board and senior management oversight to align with strategic objectives and manage all contingencies.

---
This plan satisfies Board directives while managing legal and commercial risks effectively. It serves as the blueprint for achieving strategic objectives in the Nigeria LNG contract competition.
