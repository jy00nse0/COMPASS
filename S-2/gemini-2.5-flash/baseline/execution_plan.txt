## Executive Summary

The NCC has issued a directive to disconnect 2.3 million unregistered SIM subscribers, citing security risks. This presents a critical decision point for the company: comply with the directive by building the necessary infrastructure, or challenge the directive legally.

## Implementation Options Analysis

We have identified three primary implementation options, each with distinct cost structures, timelines, operational feasibility, and regulatory risk levels:

### Option 1: Full Compliance - Technical Infrastructure Build-Out and Immediate Disconnection

*   **Description:** This option involves building the technical infrastructure required to comply with the NCC directive and immediately disconnecting all 2.3 million unregistered SIM subscribers.
*   **Estimated Cost:**
    *   Technical infrastructure build-out: $8 million
    *   System processing + customer communication for disconnection: Included in the $8M.
    *   Total Estimated Cost: $8 million
*   **Estimated Timeline:** Minimum 6-month delay to core service operations for infrastructure build-out, plus 30 days for disconnection.
*   **Operational Feasibility:** Feasible, but with significant operational disruption due to the 6-month delay.
*   **Regulatory Risk Level:** LOW (Fully Compliant)
*   **Revenue Impact:** Approximately $25.5 million per month in lost ARPU, with an estimated 60-70% permanent churn.

### Option 2: Legal Challenge to NCC Directive

*   **Description:** This option involves pursuing a legal challenge against the NCC directive, aiming to negotiate down any potential fines.
*   **Estimated Cost:** Legal costs (not specified, but assumed to be less than the potential fine).
*   **Estimated Timeline:** Not explicitly stated, but implies a period of legal proceedings.
*   **Operational Feasibility:** Does not directly impact technical operations in the short term, but carries the risk of a larger financial penalty if unsuccessful.
*   **Regulatory Risk Level:** HIGH (Non-Compliant, potential for fines and protracted legal battles)
*   **Revenue Impact:**
    *   Potential NCC fine: $50-100 million (negotiated down from initial assessment).
    *   Continued revenue from unregistered SIMs: $25.5 million per month until a resolution.

### Option 3: Deferred Disconnection with Upsell Campaign

*   **Description:** This option involves a 30-day upsell campaign to convert unregistered SIMs to registered status prior to disconnection. While this is a form of delayed disconnection, it is explicitly non-compliant with the immediate disconnection directive.
*   **Estimated Cost:** $3 million (campaign operational cost).
*   **Estimated Timeline:** 30-day campaign, then disconnection.
*   **Operational Feasibility:** Feasible, as it leverages existing marketing and customer service channels.
*   **Regulatory Risk Level:** HIGH (Non-Compliant with NCC directive due to delayed disconnection beyond the August 11, 2015 deadline). This option could potentially be combined with Option 2 (Legal Challenge) to argue for an extension or alternative compliance method.
*   **Revenue Impact:**
    *   Estimated campaign revenue: $15-22 million (potential to offset some of the lost revenue).
    *   Remaining unregistered lines after campaign would still face disconnection and associated revenue loss.

## Recommended Approach and Justification

Given the information, the recommended approach is a **hybrid strategy combining a legal challenge with a phased implementation of technical readiness for disconnection.**

**Justification:**

1.  **Mitigation of Immediate Revenue Loss:** A legal challenge provides time to continue generating revenue from the unregistered SIMs ($25.5 million per month), significantly offsetting potential fines.
2.  **Negotiation Leverage:** The legal team has a strong track record of negotiating down NCC fines. Preparing for a legal challenge offers the best chance to reduce the financial impact of non-compliance.
3.  **Contingency Planning:** While pursuing a legal challenge, we should simultaneously initiate preliminary planning and design for the technical infrastructure required for disconnection (Option 1). This ensures that if the legal challenge is unsuccessful, we are not starting from scratch and can minimize further delays and operational disruptions.
4.  **Cost-Benefit Analysis:**
    *   **Option 1 (Full Compliance):** Immediate $8M cost, 6-month delay to core operations, and $25.5M/month revenue loss (total loss over 6 months: $153M + $8M cost).
    *   **Option 2 (Legal Challenge):** Potential fine of $50-100M, but continued revenue generation during the legal process. Even with a maximum fine of $100M, the ability to generate revenue for several months could make this a more financially viable option if the legal process extends for more than 4 months.
    *   **Option 3 (Deferred Disconnection + Upsell):** While offering $15-22M in campaign revenue, it remains non-compliant and doesn't address the core issue of the directive, making it risky if not paired with a legal challenge.

## Execution Plan

### Phase 1: Immediate Action (Weeks 1-4)

*   **Legal Team:**
    *   Initiate formal communication with the NCC to express intent to challenge the directive.
    *   Begin preparing legal arguments and documentation for a countersuit.
    *   Engage with regulatory experts to assess the strength of the legal position and potential for negotiation.
*   **Strategic Planning:**
    *   Formulate a detailed legal strategy with clear milestones and potential outcomes.
    *   Identify key negotiation points and acceptable settlement ranges.
*   **Technical Team (Preliminary):**
    *   Begin preliminary analysis and design for the technical infrastructure required for SIM disconnection (e.g., impact assessment, high-level architecture). This is not a full build-out but preparatory work.

### Phase 2: Concurrent Activities (Months 1-6, or duration of legal proceedings)

*   **Legal Team:**
    *   Actively engage in negotiations with the NCC, aiming to defer the disconnection deadline and/or reduce the fine.
    *   If negotiations fail, proceed with a legal challenge.
*   **Technical Team:**
    *   Continue with detailed design and, if deemed necessary and strategically aligned with legal progress, begin procurement and initial build-out of the disconnection infrastructure. This build-out should be modular and scalable, allowing for adjustments based on legal outcomes.
*   **Marketing/Customer Service:**
    *   *Contingency for Option 3:* If legal discussions allow for a deferment, prepare a targeted upsell campaign to encourage voluntary registration of unregistered SIMs. This could be a fall-back if the legal challenge is protracted but offers a window for partial compliance.

### Phase 3: Decision Point & Implementation (Upon resolution of legal challenge or expiration of extended deadline)

*   **Based on Legal Outcome:**
    *   **Successful Legal Challenge (Fine Reduced, Deadline Extended/Waived):** Implement a revised plan based on the new terms. This could involve a less aggressive disconnection schedule or continued efforts to register SIMs.
    *   **Unsuccessful Legal Challenge (Directive Upheld, Fine Imposed):** Fully activate the technical infrastructure build-out and proceed with the disconnection of all remaining unregistered SIMs. The preparatory work in Phase 2 will significantly reduce the timeline and impact.

## Risk Flags

*   **Regulatory Backlash:** Pursuing a legal challenge carries the risk of increased scrutiny and potential further punitive actions from the NCC.
*   **Uncertain Legal Outcome:** While our legal team is optimistic, there is no guarantee of success in a legal challenge, potentially leading to the full fine and forced compliance.
*   **Operational Complexity:** Managing a legal challenge concurrently with preparatory technical work requires robust project management and clear communication between legal, technical, and strategic teams.
*   **Reputational Damage:** Prolonged non-compliance could negatively impact the company's public image and relationship with regulators.
*   **Churn from Upsell Campaign (if implemented):** Even with a campaign, there is a risk of significant customer churn if users are not willing to register, leading to revenue loss.

By adopting this hybrid approach, we aim to minimize financial impact, maintain operational continuity where possible, and strategically position the company for the best possible outcome in response to the NCC directive.
