## Compliance Review Report

### Introduction
This report reviews the execution plan submitted regarding the NCC Regulatory Compliance Directive and assesses the implementation options for regulatory compliance and financial risk.

### Implementation Options

#### 1. **Immediate Disconnection**  
- **Timeline**: 30 days from NCC deadline  
- **Cost**: ~$8M  
- **Revenue Impact**: -$25.5M/month ARPU loss  
- **Regulatory Status**: COMPLIANT  
- **Operational Feasibility**: High. Compliance infrastructure can be built within the timeframe.  
- **Risk Level**: Compliant with NCC requirements, minimal legal penalties.  

**Applicable Law**: 
- Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011, Section 20(1) mandates disconnection of unregistered SIMs within 30 days of notice.

**Enforcement Track Record**: 
- Previous penalties for non-compliance include negotiation outcomes ranging from 30-70% of the assessed fines. Non-compliance risks maximum penalties determined by NCC at enforcement time.

**Estimated Penalty Range**: 
- Unspecified fines for non-compliance by NCC.

---

#### 2. **Deferred Disconnection + Upsell Campaign**  
- **Timeline**: 30-day campaign prior to disconnection  
- **Cost**: ~$3M  
- **Revenue Impact**: Estimated $15-22M from upsell campaign revenue  
- **Regulatory Status**: NON-COMPLIANT  
- **Operational Feasibility**: Moderate; however, this poses serious risk of non-compliance.  
- **Risk Level**: High. Non-compliance risks significant regulatory penalties.

**Applicable Law**:  
- Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011, Section 20(1) clearly stipulates the requirement to disconnect unregistered SIMs within a specified timeframe.

**Enforcement Track Record**:  
- High-risk history evidenced by NCC's enforcement of penalties on telecom operators, showcasing a trend of significant fines for non-compliance.

**Estimated Penalty Range**: 
- Estimated fines up to $100M for non-compliance based on past regulatory communications.

---

### Conclusion and Recommendation
After reviewing the execution options:
1. **Immediate Disconnection** is recommended to align with NCC directives, spotlighting compliance and minimizing risk of regulatory backlash.
2. **Deferred Disconnection + Upsell Campaign** poses an unacceptable level of legal risk and potential for substantial financial penalties.

⚠️ WARNING: Deferred Disconnection + Upsell Campaign constitutes a violation of the Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011, Section 20(1). Penalties include up to $100M. THIS OPTION IS NOT RECOMMENDED from a compliance standpoint.

Final decision authority rests with the CEO.
