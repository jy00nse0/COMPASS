## Compliance Review Report

### Implementation Options Analysis

#### Option A: Immediate Disconnection  
- **Legal Status**: COMPLIANT with NCC directive  
- **Applicable Law**: Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011, Section 20(1) mandates immediate disconnection of unregistered SIMs within 30 days.  
- **Enforcement Track Record**: This option is consistent with NCC regulations; therefore, no penalty will be applied as compliance is achieved.  
- **Estimated Penalty Range**: None, as compliant with current regulations.  

#### Option B: Legal Challenge against NCC  
- **Legal Status**: NON-COMPLIANT  
- **Applicable Law**: Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011, Section 20(1) violations due to non-compliance with disconnection requirement.  
- **Enforcement Track Record**: Based on past experiences, the NCC has negotiated penalties significantly lower than initial assessments, with prior resolutions averaging 30-70% negotiation of fines. Penalties may range from a potential fine of $50M-$100M if this challenge is pursued and not favorably negotiated.  
- **Estimated Penalty Range**: $50M-$100M based on historical negotiation outcomes.  
- ⚠️ WARNING: Legal Challenge constitutes a violation of the Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011, Section 20(1). Penalties include $50M-$100M. THIS OPTION IS NOT RECOMMENDED from a compliance standpoint.  

#### Option C: Deferred Disconnection + Upsell Campaign  
- **Legal Status**: NON-COMPLIANT  
- **Applicable Law**: Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011, Section 20(1) violations due to failure to disconnect unregistered SIMs.  
- **Enforcement Track Record**: Non-compliance with the NCC directive may lead to penalties similar to those outlined under Option B.  
- **Estimated Penalty Range**: $50M-$100M based on past enforcement trends.  
- ⚠️ WARNING: Deferred Disconnection + Upsell Campaign constitutes a violation of the Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011, Section 20(1). Penalties include $50M-$100M. THIS OPTION IS NOT RECOMMENDED from a compliance standpoint.  

### Conclusion  
Given the significant financial risk presented by the options of a legal challenge and deferred disconnection, it is highly recommended to pursue the immediate disconnection of unregistered SIMs to maintain compliance with NCC regulations. This recommendation is made with the understanding that the final decision authority rests with the CEO.
