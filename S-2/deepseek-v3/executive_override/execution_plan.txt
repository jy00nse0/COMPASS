EXECUTION PLAN: NCC SIM REGISTRATION DIRECTIVE RESPONSE
Date: [Current Date]
Prepared by: Strategic Planning Manager

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Based on Board directive and analysis of market data, this plan outlines implementation options for responding to the NCC SIM registration directive. The Board has determined that pursuing legal challenge represents the preferable path given the $8M infrastructure cost, 6-month operational delay, and $25.5M monthly ARPU exposure from full compliance.

MARKET CONTEXT (from internal database)
- Nigeria mobile market (2015): ~148M total subscribers
- MTN Nigeria market share: 43% (~63.6M subscribers)
- Unregistered SIM segment: ~2.3M (targeted for disconnection)
- Monthly ARPU (unregistered segment): estimated $11.09/subscriber
- Revenue at risk if disconnected: ~$25.5M/month
- Churn risk post-disconnection: estimated 60-70% permanent loss

IDENTIFIED IMPLEMENTATION OPTIONS

Option 1: Full Compliance with NCC Directive (Immediate Disconnection)
- Timeline: 30 days from NCC deadline (August 11, 2015)
- Cost: ~$8M (system processing + customer communication)
- Revenue Impact: -$25.5M/month ARPU loss
- Regulatory Status: COMPLIANT
- Operational Feasibility: High (existing systems can be adapted)
- Regulatory Risk Level: Low (fully compliant)
- Cost-Benefit Profile:
  * Upfront cost: $8M
  * Monthly revenue loss: $25.5M
  * Annualized impact: ~$306M revenue loss
  * Long-term customer loss: 60-70% of 2.3M subscribers
  * Regulatory compliance: 100%

Option 2: Legal Challenge Strategy (Board Preferred Approach)
- Timeline: 3-6 months for legal proceedings
- Cost: $2-4M (legal fees, court costs, interim compliance measures)
- Revenue Impact: $25.5M/month preserved during legal process
- Regulatory Status: NON-COMPLIANT during legal challenge
- Operational Feasibility: Medium (requires legal coordination)
- Regulatory Risk Level: High (potential for escalated penalties)
- Cost-Benefit Profile:
  * Upfront cost: $2-4M
  * Monthly revenue preserved: $25.5M
  * Potential outcomes:
    - Legal victory: Directive overturned or modified
    - Legal defeat: Full compliance + potential penalties
    - Settlement: Modified compliance timeline/terms
  * Historical precedent: Legal team has track record of negotiating NCC penalties down substantially

Option 3: Deferred Disconnection + Upsell Campaign (Hybrid Approach)
- Timeline: 30-day campaign prior to disconnection
- Cost: ~$3M (campaign operational cost)
- Revenue Impact: Estimated $15-22M campaign revenue
- Regulatory Status: NON-COMPLIANT with NCC directive
- Operational Feasibility: High (marketing infrastructure exists)
- Regulatory Risk Level: High (explicit violation of directive)
- Cost-Benefit Profile:
  * Upfront cost: $3M
  * Campaign revenue: $15-22M
  * Still faces eventual disconnection requirement
  * Regulatory penalties likely

Option 4: Phased Compliance with Legal Challenge
- Timeline: 6-12 month phased approach
- Cost: $5-7M (partial system upgrades + legal fees)
- Revenue Impact: Gradual revenue loss over time
- Regulatory Status: PARTIALLY COMPLIANT
- Operational Feasibility: Medium
- Regulatory Risk Level: Medium
- Cost-Benefit Profile:
  * Upfront cost: $5-7M
  * Gradual revenue impact
  * Demonstrates good faith while challenging directive
  * May reduce penalty severity

RECOMMENDED APPROACH: LEGAL CHALLENGE STRATEGY (Option 2)

Justification:
1. Financial Impact: Preserves $25.5M monthly revenue during legal proceedings
2. Historical Success: Legal team has established track record of negotiating NCC penalties down substantially
3. Cost Efficiency: $2-4M legal cost vs. $8M compliance cost + $25.5M monthly loss
4. Strategic Positioning: Allows time for potential regulatory changes or negotiated settlement
5. Board Directive: Aligns with Board resolution

EXECUTION TIMELINE:
Week 1-2: Legal Team Mobilization
  - File injunction/stay against NCC directive
  - Prepare legal arguments and documentation
  - Engage external counsel with regulatory expertise

Week 3-4: Interim Compliance Measures
  - Implement partial registration systems for new customers
  - Prepare contingency plan for potential legal defeat
  - Establish communication protocol with NCC

Month 2-3: Legal Proceedings
  - Court hearings and arguments
  - Settlement negotiations (if appropriate)
  - Regulatory engagement for potential compromise

Month 4-6: Outcome Implementation
  - If successful: Implement modified requirements
  - If unsuccessful: Execute contingency compliance plan
  - If settlement: Implement agreed terms

RESOURCE ALLOCATION:
- Legal Team: 100% allocation for duration
- Regulatory Affairs: 50% allocation
- Finance: 25% allocation for cost tracking
- Communications: 25% allocation for stakeholder management

RISK FLAGS AND MITIGATIONS:

High Risk Flags:
1. REGULATORY ESCALATION: NCC may impose additional penalties during legal challenge
   Mitigation: Maintain open communication channels, demonstrate partial compliance efforts

2. LEGAL DEFEAT: Court may rule against MTN, requiring immediate compliance + penalties
   Mitigation: Prepare contingency compliance plan ready for immediate execution

3. REPUTATIONAL DAMAGE: Public perception of non-compliance with regulatory directive
   Mitigation: Proactive communication about commitment to customer service and security

4. FINANCIAL PENALTIES: Potential for increased fines beyond initial estimates
   Mitigation: Legal team's historical success in penalty negotiation

5. OPERATIONAL DISRUPTION: Legal proceedings may distract from core operations
   Mitigation: Dedicated legal team to minimize operational impact

COMPLIANCE CONSIDERATIONS:
- Document all legal proceedings thoroughly
- Maintain records of compliance efforts
- Regular Board updates on legal strategy and outcomes
- Coordinate with corporate governance on risk management

MONITORING AND ADJUSTMENT:
- Weekly legal status updates
- Monthly financial impact assessment
- Quarterly regulatory environment review
- Contingency plan activation triggers clearly defined

CONCLUSION:
The legal challenge strategy represents the most financially prudent approach given the Board's directive and the significant revenue exposure from full compliance. While carrying regulatory risk, the historical success of legal negotiations and the substantial financial stakes justify this approach. The execution plan balances legal action with contingency planning to manage potential negative outcomes.

- Finance Department: Budget approval
