EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This compliance review assesses three implementation options for the NCC SIM registration directive requiring disconnection of 2.3 million unregistered SIM subscribers by August 11, 2015. Based on regulatory analysis and enforcement precedent, Option A (Full Technical Compliance) is the only legally compliant option. Options B and C constitute regulatory violations with significant penalty exposure. Final decision authority rests with the CEO.

IDENTIFIED IMPLEMENTATION OPTIONS
1. Option A: Full Technical Compliance (Immediate Disconnection)
2. Option B: Deferred Disconnection with Upsell Campaign
3. Option C: Legal Challenge with Parallel Technical Preparation

REGULATORY FRAMEWORK ANALYSIS
Applicable Law: Registration of Telephone Subscribers Regulations, 2011
- Section 19: All subscribers must provide valid identification prior to SIM activation
- Section 20(1): Network operators shall disconnect unregistered SIMs within 30 days of regulatory notice
- Violation: Failure to disconnect = regulatory non-compliance
- Penalty: Determined by NCC at time of enforcement
- NCC deadline for current directive: August 11, 2015

ENFORCEMENT PRECEDENT ANALYSIS
Based on NCC enforcement history (MTN Nigeria):
- 2012: N360M fine → negotiated resolution, no operational disruption. Settlement at ~40% of initial assessment
- 2013: N90M fine → settled without major impact
- Pattern: NCC penalties subject to post-assessment negotiation
- Prior cases resolved at 30-70% of initial assessment
- Important Note: Current regulatory posture under new NCC leadership not yet assessed. Prior negotiation success is not guaranteed under current enforcement regime

Legal Status: COMPLIANT

CRITICAL LEGAL CONSIDERATIONS

1. Legal Challenge Does Not Stay Enforcement: The plan incorrectly assumes legal proceedings suspend the NCC directive. Regulatory enforcement typically proceeds independently of legal challenges.

2. Precedent Limitations: While prior negotiations resulted in 30-70% reductions, new NCC leadership may adopt stricter enforcement posture. The company's 43% market share creates systemic dependency that could lead to more severe penalties.

3. Timing Risk: The plan's 3-6 month legal proceedings timeline exceeds the 30-day compliance deadline, guaranteeing violation.

4. Settlement Uncertainty: The plan assumes successful fine negotiation, but current regulatory environment may limit negotiation flexibility.

COMPLIANCE RECOMMENDATIONS

1. PRIMARY RECOMMENDATION: Option A (Full Technical Compliance)
   - Eliminates regulatory risk entirely
   - Avoids potential fines of $15M-$70M
   - Maintains regulatory standing for future interactions
   - Despite significant revenue impact ($306M annually), preserves license and avoids operational sanctions

2. ALTERNATIVE CONSIDERATION: If pursuing non-compliant options, immediate engagement with NCC for extension or phased implementation
   - Formal request for deadline extension based on technical implementation challenges
   - Proposal for phased disconnection with demonstrated progress
   - Transparent communication of compliance efforts

3. RISK MITIGATION STRATEGY: If CEO selects Option B or C despite compliance warnings:
   - Establish immediate $70M reserve for potential fines
   - Prepare for license suspension contingency
   - Develop comprehensive stakeholder communication plan
   - Document all compliance efforts to demonstrate good faith
